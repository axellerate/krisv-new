Technology

Just like a writer, software engineers all have different writing styles and methods. Some prefer one language over another, some a certain environment and others even isolate themselves to specific platforms such as Android or the web. Over time, programming has become as diverse a field as any, with new ways to approach problems coming out every single week.


When I first started to get my feet wet in code, I began my career in Python - a very lovely language that is as clear as english. Although some programmers coming from Java or C++ despise Python's indentation as a way to block code, I find it very clean and easy to read. When I ran into problems, I'd often run over to Stackoverflow and reach out to the community. I remember one question in particular getting answered by the creator of Python himself, Guido Van Rossum. As a new programmer, that really surprised me! To this day, Python has a place in my heart as my favourite programming language and I love working with it.

I try to add to the list when I can. Often I find that learning a new framework or system to be a great way to pick up new ways to approach problems. Sometimes these approaches are best fit for something I may be working on, and sometimes they just help me to think in a different way. I think any software engineer will tell you that continuous learning is must-have in the world of today.
